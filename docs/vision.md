# Services Marketplace — Vision & Design

## The Problem

The current user journey for hiring a service professional is broken:

1. Google "electrician near me" → ads, SEO spam, aggregator sites
2. Maybe try GetNinjas/Thumbtack → fill out a form, get 5 strangers bidding, no real way to judge quality
3. Ask WhatsApp groups / friends → best results but slow, limited network
4. Pick someone based on vibes → hope for the best
5. Negotiate price with zero reference → no idea if it's fair
6. They show up (maybe) → no accountability on timing
7. Work gets done (maybe well) → no standardized quality check
8. Pay cash or Pix → no record, no warranty, no recourse
9. Lose their number → repeat the whole cycle next time

## Core Failures of Current Platforms

- **Lead generation disguised as marketplace.** GetNinjas sells leads, not outcomes. The professional pays to receive your contact — incentives are misaligned from day one.
- **No trust layer.** Reviews are gameable. No real verification of skill, licensing, or work history.
- **Zero relationship continuity.** Every transaction is a cold start.
- **No quality standardization.** A "5-star plumber" on one platform means nothing.
- **Professionals hate it.** They pay for leads that don't convert. Race to the bottom on pricing.

---

## Core Philosophy

**Optimize for the professional first.** If the best professionals love the platform, demand follows. Every existing marketplace gets this backwards.

---

## Key Pillars

### 1. Professional Identity That Compounds

- A **portable professional profile** that gets richer over time — not just reviews, but verified jobs completed, photos of work, certifications, response time stats, repeat client rate.
- Think LinkedIn meets a credit score for service quality.
- **The professional owns this identity.** It's their asset. This is why they stay.
- AI analyzes work photos over time to build a visual portfolio automatically.

### 2. Matching, Not Bidding

Current model: client posts job → 10 professionals bid → race to the bottom.

**Better model:**
- Client describes the problem (text, photo, voice — AI interprets).
- AI matches to **1-3 best-fit professionals** based on: specialty, location, availability, track record with similar jobs, price range.
- Client picks. No auction. No spam. Professionals only get relevant, high-intent requests.

### 3. Transparent, Fair Pricing

- AI-powered price estimation before matching: *"An electrical panel upgrade in your area typically costs R$800-1,200."*
- Professionals set their rates. Clients see the range. No negotiation anxiety.
- After the job: itemized receipt with materials vs. labor breakdown.
- Over time, the platform builds the **most accurate pricing database for services in Brazil** — that data alone is enormously valuable.

### 4. Relationship Continuity

- "Your electrician" — the platform remembers who you've used, what they did, and proactively suggests maintenance.
- *"Carlos installed your AC 11 months ago. Time for a filter cleaning? He's available Thursday."*
- Professionals build a **client book** on the platform. Recurring revenue for them. Convenience for clients.
- Turns a transactional marketplace into a **retention engine**.

### 5. Trust That's Real

- **Verified credentials:** actual license checks, insurance verification, background checks.
- **Work verification:** before/after photos, client sign-off, AI quality assessment.
- **Tiered professionals:** apprentice → verified → expert → master. Earned through platform history, not self-declared.
- **Guarantee:** if the work fails within X days, the platform covers a redo. Funded by a small fee, makes the whole ecosystem trustworthy.

### 6. The Professional's Business OS

Retention moat — give professionals tools they can't get elsewhere:

- **Schedule management** — clients book available slots directly
- **Invoicing and payments** — professional, automatic, with tax compliance
- **Materials purchasing** — group buying power for supplies (margin opportunity)
- **Financial dashboard** — "you earned X this month, Y this quarter, here's your tax estimate"
- **AI assistant** — helps estimate jobs from photos, generates quotes, manages client communication

The professional's entire business runs on the platform. Switching cost becomes enormous.

### 7. The Job Experience

- **Real-time tracking:** "Your electrician is 15 min away" (like Uber but for service calls)
- **In-job communication:** chat, photos, "I found an additional issue, here's what it looks like, approve the extra R$200?"
- **Completion flow:** client confirms work done → rates → pays through platform → automatic receipt

## The Flywheel

```
Better tools for professionals
  → Best professionals join
    → Better service for clients
      → More clients
        → More work for professionals
          → More data on pricing, quality, demand
            → Even better matching and tools
```

| Relationship | Transactional | Continuous |

## Where AI Supercharges This

- **Job interpretation:** client sends a photo of a leaking pipe → AI categorizes, estimates severity, matches specialist
- **Quality assessment:** before/after photos analyzed for work quality over time
- **Demand prediction:** "electricians in zona sul are booked 2 weeks out — opportunity to recruit more"
- **Dynamic pricing guidance:** real-time market rates based on supply/demand
- **Professional coaching:** "your quote-to-job conversion is 40%. Professionals like you average 65%. Here's what they do differently."

---

## Go-to-Market Strategy

Don't launch all categories. Pick **one vertical in one city**. Electricians in São Paulo, plumbers in BH, whatever has the most pain and volume. Nail the experience. Then expand.

**The defensible asset:** the trust graph + pricing data + professional identity system. That compounds and becomes nearly impossible to replicate.
